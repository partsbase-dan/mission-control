# PartsBase /demo Page — CRO Audit

**Page:** partsbase.com/demo  
**Goal:** Supplier demo request (highest-value acquisition conversion)  
**Traffic sources:** LinkedIn Ads, Google Search (brand + solution keywords), email nurture  
**Date:** May 2026  
**Auditor note:** This audit is based on PartsBase ICP, positioning docs, and CRO framework. Validate recommendations against live heatmap/session data once Hotjar is installed (Phase 2).

---

## Bottom Line First

The /demo page is the primary supplier acquisition gate. If it's leaking, every dollar of LinkedIn and Google spend is discounted. The three biggest likely blockers for a B2B aviation audience are: (1) unclear value proposition above the fold — aviation decision-makers are skeptical and time-poor, (2) a generic CTA that doesn't acknowledge what the demo actually shows, and (3) no objection handling for the "is this worth my 30 minutes?" question that every VP Maintenance is asking. Fix those three things before touching anything else.

---

## Dimension 1 — Value Proposition Clarity 🔴 Highest Risk

### Issue
Aviation suppliers evaluating a marketplace listing tool have a very specific question: "Will this put my inventory in front of more buyers, and is it worth switching from ILS?" The /demo page must answer that in the first 5 seconds, in language that matches how they think, not how PartsBase describes itself internally.

**Red flags to check:**
- If the hero headline uses "platform," "solution," or "marketplace" without a number, it's too vague
- "15 billion parts" is a supply-side stat — buyers care about it, but suppliers need reach and RFQ volume framing
- The key supplier value props are: **7,500+ verified buyers in 200 countries**, **51,000+ daily searches**, **PartsStore transactional listings**, **PBExpo community access**

### Quick Win
Rewrite the hero headline to lead with buyer reach, not platform description.

**Current pattern (likely):** "The World's Largest Aviation Parts Marketplace"  
**Stronger alternative A:** "Put Your Inventory in Front of 7,500 Verified Aviation Buyers — See It Live"  
**Stronger alternative B:** "51,000 Aviation Parts Searches Per Day. Book a Demo to See Who's Looking for Yours."  
**Stronger alternative C:** "The Fastest Way to Add a Global B2B Sales Channel — 15-Minute Demo"

### High-Impact Change
Add a sub-headline that names the outcome, not the feature:

> "In 15 minutes, we'll show you how distributors and OEMs on PartsBase are generating RFQs from buyers in 200 countries — without adding headcount."

## Dimension 2 — Headline Effectiveness 🟡 Medium Risk

### Issue
Message-match from LinkedIn and Google Ads is critical. If an ad says "Reach 7,500 Aviation Buyers" and the landing page headline says "Request a Demo," there's a disconnect — the visitor loses the thread and bounces.

**Check:** Does the /demo headline echo the language from the top-performing ad variants?

### Rules for the headline
- Must match UTM campaign intent (supplier acquisition, not buyer acquisition)
- Should include one specific number (buyers, countries, searches, or parts)
- Should not use the word "demo" in the headline — it's in the CTA, and using it twice in the hero reads as redundant

### Quick Wins — 3 Headline Variants to Test

| Variant | Copy | Angle |
|---|---|---|
| A | "7,500 Aviation Buyers Are Already Searching. Get in Front of Them." | Social proof + urgency |
| B | "The Aviation Parts Marketplace Trusted by Distributors in 200 Countries" | Authority + global reach |
| C | "More RFQs. Less Cold Outreach. See How PartsBase Works for Suppliers." | Pain point + outcome |

## Dimension 3 — CTA Placement, Copy, and Hierarchy 🔴 Highest Risk

### Issue
"Request a Demo" or "Book a Demo" are table-stakes — every B2B SaaS page uses them. For aviation suppliers who are busy and skeptical, the CTA needs to reduce commitment anxiety while communicating what they'll actually get.

**The objection the CTA must defeat:** "I don't want to sit through a sales pitch."

### Primary CTA rewrites

| Variant | Copy | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| A | "See How It Works for Suppliers" | Positions as information, not commitment |
| B | "Get My 15-Minute Supplier Overview" | Specific time commitment, low friction |
| C | "Show Me the Buyer Demand" | Curiosity-driven, buyer-data angle |
| D | "Book a Free Supplier Audit" | Value-add framing — they get something |

### CTA Hierarchy Rules

**Primary CTA:** One of the above — above the fold, high-contrast button color.

**Secondary CTA (optional):** "Or explore supplier plans →" — links to /pricing. Captures self-serve researchers without distracting from demo intent.

**CTA Repetition:** Repeat the primary CTA at: (1) above the fold, (2) after the social proof section, (3) at the bottom of the page. Aviation buyers scroll — don't assume above-fold capture.

### Form Friction 🔴
This is a critical area. Every extra field costs conversions.

**Recommended fields (5 max):**
1. First Name
2. Last Name
3. Business Email (not personal — filters for quality)
4. Company Name
5. What do you sell? (dropdown: Parts / Components / MRO Services / Other)

**Remove if present:** Phone number (add friction, get it in the demo), Job Title (get from LinkedIn enrichment via Clay), Company Size (enrich via Clay post-submission).

**Above the form, add:** "No commitment. 15 minutes. We'll show you real buyer search data for your part categories."

## Dimension 4 — Visual Hierarchy and Scannability 🟡 Medium Risk

### Issue
Aviation ops and procurement leaders scan before they read. The page must communicate the value in 8 seconds of scanning — headlines, numbers, logos, and a single button should carry the story without reading a word of body copy.

### Scan Path Design
1. **Hero headline** — buyer reach stat + outcome
2. **Sub-headline** — pain addressed, time commitment
3. **3-column stat bar** — "7,500+ Buyers" / "200 Countries" / "51,000 Daily Searches"
4. **Logo row** — recognized airline/MRO customer logos (social proof)
5. **Primary CTA** — high contrast, single action
6. **"How it works" section** — 3 steps with icons (optional, below fold)
7. **Testimonial** — one specific quote from a supplier, with name, title, company
8. **Repeat CTA**

### Quick Wins
- If there is currently a hero image or illustration: replace with a screenshot of the PartsBase supplier dashboard or a buyer search results view — real product screenshots outperform abstract illustrations for B2B.
- Ensure stat numbers are the largest text on the page after the headline.
- Remove any navigation links from the /demo page — this is a conversion page, not a browsing page. (Or at minimum, remove links that take users away from the conversion path.)

## Dimension 5 — Trust Signals and Social Proof 🟡 Medium Risk

### Issue
Aviation is a relationship-driven, compliance-heavy industry. Suppliers evaluating a new marketplace need to know who else is already there, that the platform is legitimate, and that the buyer demand is real — not curated.

### Trust signal hierarchy (in order of impact for this audience)

1. **Recognized customer logos** — Airlines (even regional) and major MRO shops by name. A single logo from a recognized carrier is worth more than 10 generic testimonials.
2. **Specific testimonial with numbers** — "We went from 20 RFQs/month to 85 RFQs/month in our first 90 days on PartsBase." — [Name, Title, Company]
3. **Third-party validation** — Any aviation industry press mention, award, or certification badge (AS9120, FAA repair station customers, etc.)
4. **Platform stats** — "15B+ parts listed" / "7,500+ verified buyers" / "51,000 daily searches" — real numbers presented as stats, not claims
5. **Since [year]** — Longevity matters in aviation. "Trusted since [founding year]" signals stability.

### Quick Wins
- If there are no supplier testimonials on the page currently, add one placeholder in the design and make sourcing a real quote a P0 task.
- Place the logo row directly below the hero — before the form — to build credibility before the ask.

## Dimension 6 — Objection Handling 🟡 Medium Risk

### The Top 5 Supplier Objections to /demo

Aviation suppliers who land on this page have specific objections that must be addressed on the page itself — not left for the demo call.

| Objection | Where to Address |
|---|---|
| "We're already on ILS — why switch?" | FAQ or feature comparison table. Position as additive, not replacement. "Most of our top suppliers list on both ILS and PartsBase — they're not the same buyers." |
| "Is the buyer demand real, or just inflated stats?" | Inline with the stat bar: "All buyer data is verified and updated daily. We'll show you real search volume for your specific part categories in your demo." |
| "How much does it cost?" | FAQ: "Plans start at $X/month. We'll walk through pricing in the demo — there's no commitment to proceed." (Exact number TBD from /pricing.) |
| "How long does setup take?" | FAQ or "How it works" section: "Most suppliers have their inventory live and searchable within 48 hours." |
| "We tried a marketplace before and got low-quality leads." | Testimonial: Choose a quote that specifically references RFQ quality, not just volume. |

### Implementation
Add a compact FAQ section (accordion, 4–5 questions) below the form. Keep each answer under 2 sentences — this is not a support doc, it's an objection deflector.

## Dimension 7 — Friction Points 🔴 Highest Risk

### Known/Likely Friction Sources

**Form fields:** See Dimension 3. Every field beyond 5 costs ~10% of completions in B2B.

**Page navigation:** If the /demo page retains the main site navigation, users are one click away from leaving to browse the site. On a paid traffic landing page, this is conversion leak. Consider a conversion-only layout (no nav) for the LinkedIn Ads and Google Ads traffic specifically — use UTM-based conditional nav removal if needed.

**Mobile experience:** LinkedIn Ads traffic skews mobile. The form must be thumb-friendly, and the CTA must be visible without scrolling on a 390px viewport. Test on iPhone SE.

**Load speed:** Aviation procurement staff are often on airport WiFi or hotel connections. Every second of load time over 3 seconds costs measurable conversion. Compress hero images, defer non-critical scripts.

**CookiePro consent banner:** The consent overlay fires on page load. It interrupts the above-fold experience. Ensure the banner is compact, non-modal, and auto-dismisses on interaction — do not let it cover the form or CTA.

**"Thank you" page:** After form submission, do not leave users on a generic "Thanks, we'll be in touch" page. Deliver immediate value:
- Confirm demo is booked with calendar invite link (if using Calendly/HubSpot meetings)
- Link to a relevant asset: "While you wait — download our [Supplier Onboarding Guide / AOG Sourcing Checklist]"
- This thank-you page should also fire the `demo_requested` GA4 event AND the HubSpot workflow trigger

## Paid Traffic Message-Match Check

Before any new LinkedIn or Google campaign goes live, verify the following chain:

```
Ad headline → /demo page headline → Form confirmation → Thank-you page
```

Each step should feel like a continuation, not a new experience. If an ad says "Reach 7,500 Aviation Buyers," the page headline should echo "7,500 Buyers" — not start fresh with brand language.

**For the q3-2026-linkedin-mro-demo campaign specifically:**
- LinkedIn intro copy angle → should land on a headline that continues that angle
- Do not use a generic /demo URL for all campaigns — consider UTM-specific headline variations (easily done via a CMS or GTM-based headline swap)
