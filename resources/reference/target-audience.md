# Target Audience Overview

---

## The Aviation Supply Chain

The aviation supply chain involves the manufacturing, distribution, procurement, and maintenance of aircraft, aviation systems, and aerospace products.

PartsBase targets companies in **all areas** of the aviation supply chain. PartsBase offers supply-chain management solutions for companies in the aviation industry, connecting buyers and sellers of aviation parts and components in a global marketplace.


### Supply Chain Flow

| Stage | Examples |
|-------|----------|
| **Raw Material Suppliers** | Metal, plastic, adhesives, sealants, fasteners, fluids, seals, bearings, electrical |
| **Multi-Tier Suppliers** | Motors, hydraulic pumps, components, and parts |
| **1st Tier Suppliers** | Structures, systems, interiors, engineering, design, logistics | 
| **OEM** |  Aircraft, engines, avionics, systems, parts, components |
| **MROs** | Maintenance, repair, overhaul |
| **Consumers** | Airlines, States |

## Who Is Our Target Audience?

| Segment | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Suppliers / Distributors** | Businesses that provide aircraft parts, components, and materials to various segments of the aviation industry. |
| **MROs** | Companies that specialize in maintaining, repairing, and overhauling aircraft to ensure their airworthiness and safety. |
| **Charter & Corporate Flight** | Entities offering on-demand air transportation services for individuals, businesses, and organizations, including private charters and corporate flight services. |
| **FBOs (Fixed Base Operators)** | Facilities providing a range of aviation services, including fueling, hangar space, ground handling, and passenger amenities, to private and corporate aircraft operators. |
| **AMOs (Approved Maintenance Organizations)** | Organizations authorized by aviation authorities to perform maintenance, repair, and overhaul activities on aircraft and related components. |
| **Airlines** | Companies that operate scheduled passenger and cargo flights, providing transportation services to passengers and freight customers. |
| **Contract Manufacturers** | Businesses that specialize in manufacturing aerospace components, parts, and assemblies on a contractual basis for OEMs and other clients in the aviation industry. |
| **Prime Manufacturers** | Manufacture completed aircraft or engines supplied to a purchaser(s) or operator(s) of such aircraft or engines for flight operations. |
| **OEMs (Original Equipment Manufacturers)** | Companies responsible for designing, manufacturing, and selling aircraft, engines, avionics, and other aerospace systems to customers worldwide. |
| **Brokers** | Individuals who act as intermediaries between buyers and sellers, facilitating transactions without taking ownership of products. |
| **Others** | Aircraft leasing, Flight schools, Consultants |

## Ideal Customer Profiles

An **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)** is a detailed description of a hypothetical perfect customer. We use Ideal Customer Profiles to identify and qualify leads efficiently, targeting companies that will get us closer to our KPIs.

The ICP focuses on the business profile and is based on market research and real customer data to describe concrete traits that our ideal customer should have.

**Key factors:**
- Target company size (workforce, revenue)
- Target company business model

### Examples

| Buyer ICP | Seller ICP |
|-----------|------------|
| Operator or MRO | Distributor |
| 51–200 employees | 1,000+ employees |
| $20M–$50M revenue | $500M+ revenue |
| Fleet of 10+ aircraft | Holds inventory |
| Own maintenance service | The buyer is also the end-user |
| Has inventory | |

### The PartsBase Ideal Customer Profiles (ICP)

| Tier | Company Size | Revenue | Years in Business |
|------|--------------|---------|-------------------|
| **Small Business** | 11–200 employees | $5M+ | 3+ |
| **Mid-Market** | 200–1K employees | $20M+ | 10+ |

- Own a maintenance team or offer third-party maintenance service

## Customer Personas

A persona is a representation of the people that buy a product or service. We use customer personas to know who we are selling to and what will influence their buying decision.

The goal of a buyer persona is to help sellers identify and qualify leads, understanding why they need our solution and how they make decisions.

**A persona is created by looking at:**
- Customer demographics
- Firmographic data
- Purchasing behavior
- Customer interviews
- Discovery calls

**What makes a PartsBase persona?**
- Name, age, role
- Qualities, characteristics, behavior
- Industry, company size, revenue
- Procurement goals
- Procurement challenges/needs

## Anti-Persona: "Red Flag Ryan"

**This is NOT our customer** Disqualifying signals:

- No aircraft

- Start-up with no industry experience and no revenue
- No inventory as a seller
- No registered company
- Agriculture / Russian / Ultralight / Drones
- No website / presence
- P.O. Box or residential address
- On the blacklist
