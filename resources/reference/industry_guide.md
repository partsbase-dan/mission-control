## 001 — Industry Overview

The aviation and aerospace industry is a global market involving the design, production, maintenance, and operation of aircraft and related systems.

The industry operates within a complex global supply chain network, involving parts and components manufacturers, distributors, repair facilities, airlines, regulatory authorities, and many other stakeholders.

Coordination among these entities is essential to ensure the efficiency of the supply-chain and to maintain a healthy, profitable market. The safety and reliability of aircraft systems, their passengers, and operators all over the world, also rely on this cross-industry alignment.

PartsBase lands at the center of the aviation supply-chain network, streamlining procurement and fulfillment process for thousands of organizations worldwide.

**Here's how:**

- Millions of parts and components go into building aircraft and related systems.
- These parts and components are manufactured and distributed by many types of businesses.
- These parts and components are also purchased, either to be used or resold, by many other companies.
- Parts and components often need to be repaired, maintained, or overhauled. Many certified businesses offer these services.
- PartsBase's PBLocator connects buyers and sellers of parts and components in a central marketplace.
- PBLocator also allows repair and overhaul organizations to list capabilities and receive service requests through the platform.

## 003 — Industry Trends & Growth Factors

*Major forces currently shaping the industry worldwide.*

- Higher demand for commercial aircraft, especially in emerging markets with rising middle-class populations and urbanization levels.
- Elevated global defense spending as geopolitical tensions persist. Governments worldwide prioritize enhancing military capabilities.
- Sustainability is a growing concern in the industry. Many organizations are shifting towards more fuel-efficient aircraft.
- The need for MRO services is expected to grow as operational efficiency remains a priority for industry players worldwide.
- The industry is gradually improving after the downturns caused by the Covid-19 pandemic as air passenger travel, cargo operations, and aircraft manufacturing rise.
- Increasing competition amongst airlines to provide low-cost travel solutions and positive travel experiences.
- Increasing cuts in spending and manufacturing costs due to the market stagnation caused by Covid and demand for low-cost travel.
- Emerging technologies such as electric propulsion, autonomous aircraft, additive manufacturing (3D printing), and AI are reshaping the future of aviation and aerospace.
- AI and IoT technologies play increasingly critical roles in driving innovation, efficiency, and safety across various aspects of aviation operations, maintenance, and air traffic management.

## 004 — Regulatory Bodies & Certifications

*The entities responsible for setting safety standards and regulations.*

National and foreign regulatory bodies are responsible for setting safety standards and regulations for aircraft design, manufacturing, operations, maintenance, and air traffic management.

PartsBase customers are encouraged to list certifications, but not required to. They are also subject to on-site audits and inventory inspections.

### Key Regulatory Bodies & Certifications

- **FAA** — The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) is responsible for regulating civil aviation in the United States.
- **EASA** — The European Aviation Safety Agency (EASA) carries out regulatory efforts across 31 European countries.
- **CAAC** — The Civil Aviation Administration of China oversees civil aviation in the People's Republic of China.
- **ISO9001** — Globally recognized standard for quality management.
- **ASA-100** — Certification issued by the Aviation Suppliers Association that focuses on impartiality, competence, and reliability.
- **AS9120** — Certification based on standards set by the Society of Automotive Engineers (SAE).

## 008 — The Procurement & Fulfillment Game

*Challenges and objections we must navigate with customers and prospects.*

### Buyers want transparency & trust

Buyers want to find the best value for their money and prioritize suppliers who can consistently offer fair market prices. When suppliers omit or repeatedly alter prices, PartsBase loses part of its value for Buyers.

### Sellers want to offer competitive pricing

Many times, suppliers will not list the price of a given part or component on PartsBase because their pricing strategy is dynamic, meaning that prices change according to external factors like current market demand, the season, supply changes and price bounding.

### How do we solve that?

PartsBase offers solutions and incentives for Sellers to openly list prices on the platform.

- **Automation:** Customers can integrate PartsBase with ERP systems to automate inventory updates, including pricing information.
- **Top ranking:** The PartsBase algorithm prioritizes listings with the most data, especially pricing and images. Achieving top search rank increases a Seller's chance of being noticed and receiving RFQs.
- **Retention:** We consistently remind and show customers that building a relationship of trust and transparency with Buyers can result in long-term partnerships and higher customer retention.

## 009 — What Are Customers Selling or Buying?

*The different categories of parts & components and what they mean.*

### Expendables

Spare parts that once used, cannot be re-used, overhauled or repaired.

*Examples:* Nuts, bolts, gaskets, etc.

### Consumables

Generally bulk-type materials called out in maintenance and repair procedures for aircraft, engines, equipment, and component end items.

*Examples:* Air filters, cements, paint, lubricants, etc.

### Rotables

Component or inventory item that can be repeatedly and economically restored to a fully serviceable condition. A rotable part/component is serialized and is also a part that can be rebuilt or overhauled.

*Examples:* Starters, avionics, hydraulic pumps, valves, etc.

### Avionics

Electrical and electronic devices used in aircraft or control systems, such as navigation and communication devices.

*Examples:* Electronic systems.

### Airframe Parts

Basic structure of an airplane or spacecraft excluding its engine and instrumentation; its principal components.

*Examples:* Wings, fuselage, assembly, landing gear, etc.

PBLocator and ILS Marketplace are the largest aviation parts & components marketplaces in the world. Many companies leverage both platforms or switch from one to the other.

### PartsBase vs ILS

Founded in 1979, ILS was the first procurement & fulfillment software for aviation parts and components.

- It's now owned by CAMP Systems, a group that owns Quantum ERP.

ILS products include:

- A marketplace for parts, components, and services

- An API/integration platform

### Other relevant competitors for PBLocator

- **Locatory** — The largest aviation marketplace in the eastern hemisphere and the third largest in the world.
- **StockMarket.aero** — The largest free open marketplace for aircraft parts and components. A subsidiary of CAMP Systems, the company that also owns ILS.
- **Aeroxchange** — An online network for the MRO industry founded by 13 major global airlines in 2000. Not a software.

### What are the key trends and challenges in the aviation industry?

Key trends in the aviation industry include advancements in technology, sustainability initiatives, increasing demand for air travel, and the rise of electric and hybrid aircraft. Challenges include supply chain disruptions, regulatory compliance, and the need for digital transformation.

### How does the aviation supply chain work, and where does PartsBase fit into it?

The aviation supply chain involves the procurement, manufacturing, distribution, and maintenance of aircraft and aerospace products. PartsBase acts as a central marketplace, connecting buyers and sellers to streamline procurement processes, manage inventory, and facilitate transactions within the supply chain.

### What regulations and compliance standards do aviation companies need to adhere to sell parts and provide maintenance and overhaul services?

Aviation companies must comply with regulations from organizations such as the FAA (Federal Aviation Administration) and EASA (European Union Aviation Safety Agency). PartsBase supports compliance by providing access to certified parts, traceability features, and compliance documentation.

### What is the difference between a broker and a distributor?

A broker acts as an intermediary between buyers and sellers, facilitating transactions without taking ownership of the products. They typically earn a commission on sales. A distributor purchases products from manufacturers and resells them to customers, often maintaining inventory and providing additional services such as logistics and customer support.

### What is the difference between an FBO and an AMO?

An FBO (Fixed-Base Operator) provides a range of services to general aviation aircraft, including fueling, hangar space, maintenance, catering, and passenger amenities. An AMO (Approved Maintenance Organization) is a maintenance organization approved by aviation regulatory authorities to perform maintenance, repair, and overhaul activities on aircraft and aircraft components. While both provide maintenance services, an FBO offers a broader range of services beyond maintenance, catering to general aviation needs.

### What is the difference between an MRO and an OEM?

An MRO (Maintenance, Repair, and Overhaul) company specializes in providing maintenance services for aircraft and aerospace components, ensuring their safe operation and airworthiness. An OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) designs, manufactures, and sells aircraft, aerospace products, and components. While an MRO focuses on maintaining and repairing existing aircraft and components, an OEM produces new aircraft and original equipment.

### What sets PartsBase apart from competitors in the aviation industry?

PartsBase offers a user-friendly platform with a comprehensive parts database and advanced procurement tools. Our focus on customer service, industry expertise, and commitment to innovation sets us apart from competitors.

### What are PartsBase's main competitors?

PartsBase's main competitor is ILS (Inventory Locator Service), a US based company that also offers a platform to sell aviation parts and services. Other smaller competitors include Locatory, Stockmarket.aero, and Aeroxchange.

## 014 — Glossary

**AMO (Approved Maintenance Organization):** A maintenance organization approved by aviation regulatory authorities to perform maintenance, repair, and overhaul activities on aircraft and aircraft components.

**API (Application Programming Interface):** A way for two or more computer programs or components to communicate with each other. It is a type of software interface, offering a service to other pieces of software.

**Compliance:** Adherence to regulatory requirements, industry standards, and customer specifications related to product quality, safety, and environmental impact.

**Capabilities:** The range of skills and resources possessed by repair organizations to perform services on specific parts and components.

**Contract Manufacturing:** The outsourcing of manufacturing activities to third-party suppliers or manufacturers to produce parts or components according to specified requirements.

**EDI (Electronic Data Interchange):** The electronic exchange of business documents, such as purchase orders, invoices, and shipping notices, between trading partners in a standardized format.

**ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning):** A software system used by organizations to manage and integrate core business processes, including finance, human resources, supply chain management, and manufacturing.

**FBO (Fixed-Base Operator):** An organization that provides a range of services to general aviation aircraft, including fueling, hangar space, maintenance, catering, and passenger amenities.

**Fulfillment:** The process of processing and delivering customer orders, including picking, packing, shipping, and tracking, to ensure timely and accurate delivery.

**IoT (Internet of Things):** Refers to a network of interconnected devices, sensors, machines, and objects that communicate and exchange data over the internet without requiring human intervention.

**Inventory:** The stock of parts, components, and materials held by an organization for use in manufacturing, maintenance, or repair operations.

**Integration:** The process of combining or coordinating different systems, processes, or components to work together seamlessly and efficiently.

**JIT (Just-in-Time) Inventory:** An inventory management strategy aimed at minimizing inventory holding costs by ordering parts or components only when needed for production or maintenance.

**Lead Time:** The time it takes for a supplier to deliver ordered parts or components, including processing, production, and shipping time.

**Logistics:** The management of the flow of materials, parts, and finished products through the supply chain, including transportation, warehousing, and inventory management.

**MRO (Maintenance, Repair, and Overhaul):** Companies that provide maintenance, repair, and overhaul services for aircraft and aerospace systems.

**OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer):** A company that designs, manufactures, and sells aircraft or aerospace products.

**Outsourcing:** The practice of contracting out certain business functions or processes to external suppliers or service providers to reduce costs or focus on core competencies.

**Overhaul:** Comprehensive inspection, maintenance, and repair of aircraft components or systems to ensure airworthiness and performance.

**Procurement:** The process of acquiring goods or services from external suppliers, including sourcing, purchasing, and contract negotiation.

**Quality Assurance (QA):** The systematic process of ensuring that products or services meet established quality standards and customer requirements.

**Reverse Logistics:** The process of managing the return, repair, refurbishment, or disposal of products, parts, or materials from customers or end-users back through the supply chain.

**RFQ (Request for Quote):** A formal request sent to suppliers to provide pricing and availability information for specific parts or components.

**Supplier:** A company that provides parts, components, materials, or services to OEMs, MROs, or other entities in the aviation industry.

**Supplier Relationship Management (SRM):** The strategic management of relationships with suppliers to optimize value, minimize risk, and enhance collaboration and innovation.

**Supply Chain:** The network of organizations, activities, and resources involved in the production, distribution, and delivery of goods and services to customers.

**Traceability:** The ability to track and trace the history, location, and usage of parts or components throughout the supply chain, ensuring compliance with regulations and quality standards.
