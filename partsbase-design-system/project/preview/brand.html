Background clearance

Give the logo at least one x-height of clear space on all sides. Use the light-background mark on white and tints; use the dark-background mark (white "Parts") on navy color-800 or imagery. Avoid placing either mark on mid-blue — the blue swoosh collides with the background.

✓ On white
✓ On tint — color-100
✓ On navy — color-800
✓ On deep navy / imagery

Don't

✗ Either mark on mid-blue
✗ On pale blue — low contrast
✗ Reduced opacity
✗ Busy gradients

Voice & content

Short, declarative, domain-aware. Never cutesy — customers are airlines, MROs, and defense buyers.

Do

Be direct about the value. Lead with the outcome, follow with the mechanic. Use industry terms (MRO, OEM, FBO, BOM, PMA) — the audience expects them.

  • "Search, compare, and connect with trusted suppliers."
  • "Buy ready-to-send parts at the click of a button."
  • "Serving 6,000+ aerospace companies in 140+ countries."

Don't

No startup-speak, no emoji, no exclamation marks. Don't explain what aerospace is — the reader already works in it.

  • "Unlock aerospace superpowers 🚀"
  • "The Uber for airplane parts"
  • "Built for the modern aviation hustler"
